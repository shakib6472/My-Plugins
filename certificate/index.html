ACKNOWLEDGEMENT OF UNIVERSAL PRECAUTIONS
Infection control in the health care delivery setting is an important component of your job as a PA. This is vital for the individuals you care for as well as for your own health and well-being. The following "Universal/Standard Precautions" represent wise practices that are applied for prevention if transmission of blood borne infections such as AIDS, and HEPAPITIS B occurs. These practices are to be used when caring for all patients, not just those thought to be at risk.

1. Wash hands after contact with body substances before clean or sterile invasive procedures, and before eating or preparing food.

2. Wear gloves for direct contact with anybody substances (pus, sputum, urine, feces, blood, saliva or when starting an IV, taking blood specimens, removing a patient from a bedpan, handling concarmoited linens, suctioning a patient, caring for an open wound, changing or dressing or if you have an open lesion on your hand).

3. Wear a gown or moisture repellant apron when soiling is expected such as when a patient has diarrhea or if changing or dressing to an open wound.

4. Use masks and eye-coverings when providing direct care to the patient during suctioning, taking blood or working with IV's, changing TPN or other central line dressings, whenever mucous membranes exposure is expected or in the presence of known or suspected diseases spread by respiratory route.

5. Wash exposed portions of body and exposed clothing as soon as possible if unprotected contact with blood and body fluids.

6. Disposable syringes and needles, scalpels, blades or other sharp objects should be placed in special puncture resistant containers near the usage area. To prevent needle stick injuries, needles should not be recapped, purposefully bent, broken or manipulated by hand.
